Mites

A dark comedy including a widow, a talking cat and a pest control expert who’s not to be trusted.

A new darkly comic, absurdist play by James Mannion, Mites is a sinister exploration of the manipulation that lies beneath relationships, in particular of those who are mentally vulnerable in society.A lonely woman, abandoned by her husband, lives in an isolated house with her outspoken, anthropomorphic cat, Bartholomew.

One day she is visited by Ken, a Pest Controller, who claims to be her ex-husband returned to her. Deceived by his lies and obsessed with memories of the past, the woman accepts Ken into her life, despite the sceptical protestations of Bartholomew.

As her self-deception grows and Ken's true intentions become clear, how will she survive the competitive machinations of her two male companions? And is there more to Bartholomew than meets the eye?

Reviews

It sounds so hyperbolic that it is almost fantastical. But that’s where the beauty of this piece lies. James Mannion turns his hand well to this psychological masterpiece. It is a ruthless assault on the senses but beautifully interwoven for this three hander.
